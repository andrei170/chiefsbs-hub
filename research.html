2. Niche category and regulated-finance frame

The business-funding-education category sits inside the regulated-finance attention surface in 2026. Three live infrastructure facts:

  1. The FTC and CFPB are actively targeting business-funding-coach claims that imply guaranteed funding outcomes, specific dollar amounts within specific timelines, or representations about lender approval that bypass independent underwriting.
  2. Schema.org has FinancialProduct, FinancialService, and Course markup types that allow legitimate educational operators to mark up their offers with explicit educational-scope, eligibility, and risk-disclosure structured data. Most funding-education operators in this category are not deploying them.
  3. Independent lender underwriting is the actual gate. Funding eligibility depends on creditworthiness, business documentation, time-in-business, revenue evidence, and the underwriting standards of independent lenders. Education does not change that gate.

The infrastructure AG1 builds for any operator in this category is calibrated around the legitimate educational scope of the offer: the curriculum, the buyer self-assessment, the lender-relationship walkthrough, the credit-profile build-out education, the documentation-readiness work. None of those require AG1 to enter the funding-provision chain. All of them benefit from the SEO + AEO + landing + email infrastructure AG1 already builds for info-product operators.

3. The four observable funnel and visibility gaps

The audit below lists the four observable structural gaps that map to AG1's actual service catalog. Bottleneck-analysis.md ranks them and adds the dedicated SEO and AEO section.

3.1 No VSL on the chiefsbs.com surface

The audience that watches Mr. Brown across 804K IG followers and the YouTube channel currently lands on a static page with no operator-voice pre-sell video calibrated to the educational scope of the offer. The buy decision is made on static page elements plus the third-party trust-signal context the visitor brings with them from Scam Detector and the wider commentary surface. The fix is a 5-7 minute VSL filmed by Mr. Brown in an instructive-educator register, calibrated to the regulated-finance scope: what the curriculum teaches, who the curriculum is for, what the buyer is responsible for, what the independent lender is responsible for, and the explicit risk and outcome variability statement.

3.2 No qualification scorecard between the audience and the offer

Anyone with a card and a checkout intent currently enters the offer at the same friction as a buyer who has built business credit for 3 years. This is the upstream router gap. A 10-12 question Business Funding Readiness Scorecard (time-in-business, revenue band, current personal credit band, current business credit established yes / no, EIN age, documentation readiness, current capital need band, current lender relationships, prior funding-coach experience, exit goal) returns Too Early, Foundation-Building Track, Mentorship-Ready Track, or Advanced-Operator Track, plus a 3-line bottleneck readout. Captures email. Tags by readiness band. The scorecard filters fit-mismatched buyers before checkout, lifts review quality at the source, and creates the segmented email asset the current funnel does not capture.

3.3 No segmented welcome flow after the buy event

Any buyer who completes the purchase currently routes into the same downstream as any other buyer. No buyer-tier segmentation, no readiness-band-based onboarding, no documentation-readiness checklist sequence, no expectations reset on what the curriculum teaches versus what independent lenders decide. The fix is the AG1-built segmented welcome flow: webhook handler on the buy event, tag by readiness band, fork the welcome sequence per band, route the entry buyer into the foundation-track onboarding and the advanced-track buyer into the operator-track onboarding.

3.4 The four surfaces (IG, YouTube, chiefsbs.com, the secondary handle) do not route to a unified hub

The audience that lands looking for the curriculum, the audience that lands looking for the free training, and the audience that lands looking for the testimonial wall currently route through four separate surfaces with no unified hub that surfaces all of them in one consolidated AG1-style routing layer. The fix is the unified hub on a Mr. Brown-owned subdomain (or chiefsbs.com itself) that surfaces the offer, the free training, the named-alumni receipts wall, the schema-marked-up About section, the FAQ wall, and the legal-scope footer in one canonical surface.
