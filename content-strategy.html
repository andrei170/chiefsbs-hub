The one-sentence read

The 804K-IG audience and the YouTube channel are already producing top-of-funnel attention. The AG1-built scorecard becomes the single mid-funnel router that every existing content surface points to. No new content load. No platform shift. The existing content engine becomes the acquisition layer for the segmented email asset and the readiness-band-routed buy decision.

The unified routing layer

Every existing content surface routes to one canonical mid-funnel asset: the Business Funding Readiness Scorecard at chiefsbs.com/readiness (URL locks day 1).

The router script

  • IG link-in-bio: swaps to chiefsbs.com/readiness
  • IG Story swipe-up / link sticker: chiefsbs.com/readiness with UTM source ig-story
  • IG bio CTA copy: locks in copy-strategy.md (educational-scope register, no income claims)
  • YouTube description (every video, going forward): "Take the free Business Funding Readiness Score: chiefsbs.com/readiness" with UTM source yt-description
  • YouTube pinned comment (every video, going forward): same CTA, same UTM
  • YouTube end card: routes to chiefsbs.com/readiness
  • YouTube channel banner CTA: routes to chiefsbs.com/readiness
  • chiefsbs.com hero CTA: Take the Readiness Score (not Buy Now, not Apply Now - the scorecard is the upstream router)
  • chiefsbs.com offer-page entry (existing): preserved, but the recommended path becomes the scorecard

Result: every external attention surface points to one canonical asset that captures email, tags by readiness band, and routes the buyer to the right downstream.

What the content surfaces themselves do NOT change

  • No tone shift on @chiefsbs IG. The audience-facing register stays Mr. Brown's. AG1 does not script Mr. Brown's IG voice.
  • No new content production schedule beyond the VSL film day.
  • No content removal request. Existing content stays live. The router layer sits underneath.
  • No request to change the YouTube content format. The long-form Tyrell Brown channel content continues. Description + pinned comment routing is the only ask.

Three content pieces AG1 owns post-install

These are AG1-produced assets that live on the AG1-built surfaces (hub, landing, FAQ wall). They are not Mr. Brown content production load.

1. The 5-7 minute VSL on chiefsbs.com

Educational-scope register. Script in vsl-preview.md. Mr. Brown is on camera for one film day. AG1 scripts, directs the record, edits the cut, embeds across surfaces.

2. The FAQ wall

Educational-scope answers to the 4 high-intent AEO queries identified in bottleneck-analysis.md plus the natural buyer-readiness questions surfaced by the scorecard. Schema-marked-up with FAQPage structured data. Lives on the AG1 hub.

3. The named-alumni testimonials wall

AG1 builds the wall. Mr. Brown's team supplies the consent-cleared alumni names, headshots, and educational-outcome quotes (curriculum-completion language, learning-outcome language, documentation-readiness language - not funding-outcome language). AG1 will draft template alumni-quote prompts on day 1.

What is explicitly off-table

  • No paid ad campaign in the first 7 days. The install is organic-funnel infrastructure. Paid ads layer over the install only after the scorecard and the segmented welcome flow are live and producing data.
  • No partnership or affiliate program in the first 7 days. The install focuses on the owned-surface infrastructure first.
  • No platform shift (no TikTok build, no LinkedIn build, no X build). The existing surfaces stay; the router layer is the only change.
  • No content production calendar. Mr. Brown's existing content cadence is the input the install compounds on.

Year-1 compounding read

The router layer goes live in less than 7 days from kickoff. The compounding curve is on the 6-12 month timeline: every IG post, every YouTube upload, every Story, every podcast appearance that routes traffic to chiefsbs.com from day 7 forward is now feeding the scorecard, building the segmented email list, and feeding the schema-marked-up trust-signal infrastructure that compounds on the AEO citation surface.
