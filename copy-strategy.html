The two registers in play

Two distinct copy registers operate side by side. They do not compete and they do not overlap.

Register Owned by Surface Tone
Audience-facing voice Mr. Brown @chiefsbs IG, YouTube long-form, third-party podcast appearances Identity-forward, declarative, high-energy, claim-heavy
Educational-scope voice AG1 (built) chiefsbs.com hub, landing, scorecard, FAQ wall, email sequences, post-purchase onboarding Instructive, calm, specific, regulated-niche-disciplined

The audience-facing register pulls attention. The educational-scope register holds the buyer through the consideration and post-purchase phases without surfacing claim language that triggers FTC, CFPB, chargeback, or third-party trust-signal compounding risk. AG1 builds only the educational-scope register. The audience-facing register stays Mr. Brown's.

The five hard bans across every AG1-built surface

These bans apply to every AG1-produced word on chiefsbs.com, the landing, the scorecard, the FAQ wall, the email sequences, the SMS sequences, and the onboarding flow.

  1. No funding-outcome claims. No dollar amounts. No timelines. No "Get $X in Y days" framing. Funding eligibility depends on the underwriting standards of independent lenders. The curriculum teaches the buyer; the lender approves the funding.
  2. No reproduction of surface bio claims. "Trillionaire", "legally rob the banks", "10 million plus funded" claim, "$50K to $100K in 7 days" - none of these appear in any AG1-built surface. The audience-facing register can carry them; the educational-scope register cannot.
  3. No income claims of any kind. Curriculum-completion language and educational-outcome language only. The buyer learns to read business credit, not the buyer earns $X.
  4. No "fix your credit" or "guaranteed approval" or "bypass underwriting" framing. Underwriting is the gate. Education does not change the gate.
  5. No em-dashes (U+2014). Use hyphen, colon, or sentence break. Sign-off "- Andrei" only.

The five hard requirements across every AG1-built surface

  1. Regulated-finance footer disclaimer on every surface. Locked language: "Educational content only. Not financial, legal, or tax advice. Outcomes vary. Funding eligibility depends on creditworthiness and underwriting standards of independent lenders."
  2. Educational scope language in every CTA. "Take the Readiness Score", "Apply to the curriculum", "Get the foundation-track checklist" - the verb is educational, not funding-outcome.
  3. Named-alumni testimonials only. No first-name-only, no initials, no anonymous receipts. Consent-cleared, headshot-attached, educational-outcome quotes.
  4. Schema-marked-up FAQ on every educational-scope answer. FAQPage schema is the AEO lever.
  5. Risk-language in plain English in every long-form surface. The VSL carries the explicit risk statement on camera. The landing carries it in the FAQ. The email sequence carries it in the welcome.

Tone, cadence, and sentence-level rules for AG1-built copy

  • Sentence cadence: short to medium. The instructive-educator register reads cleanly out loud. Hemingway grade 6.
  • Paragraph length: 2-4 sentences. No wall-of-text.
  • Voice: second person ("you") in landing and email. Third-person observational ("the buyer", "the operator") in research, bottleneck-analysis, and FAQ wall content where neutral framing reads as professional.
  • Numbers: specific where verifiable (804K IG, 5,497 following, 4,023 posts). Hedged where projected ("the install compounds on the existing baseline", not "you will get $X").
  • Cohort or program pricing: language only on the landing ("low-mid-five-figure range", "premium pricing, application-based"). Specific numbers only appear in the email body when the buyer surfaces them via the scorecard.
