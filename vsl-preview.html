VSL Preview Script - For Mr. Brown (@chiefsbs)

Prepared: 2026-05-17 Scope: Section-by-section script for the 5-7 minute pre-sell VSL on chiefsbs.com. Register: Operator-educator. Educational-scope only. Zero income claims. Zero "trillionaire" or "legally rob the banks" or "7-day funding" language. Speaker: Mr. Brown, on camera, one film day. Length target: 5 to 7 minutes.

Open the conversation

Section 1 - The cold-frame open (40 to 60 seconds)

If you are here, you have probably already seen me on Instagram or YouTube. Before you do anything else on this page, watch this short walkthrough. I am going to tell you what the curriculum teaches, who it is for, what you are responsible for, what the lender is responsible for, and what outcomes vary on.

If any one of those lines does not line up with where you are, you can close the page and come back when it does. I want the right buyer in the curriculum, not the wrong one.

What the curriculum teaches

Section 2 - The educational scope (60 to 90 seconds)

The curriculum teaches four things.

One: how business credit works at the underwriting level, not the surface level. What the lender actually reviews, what scores carry weight, what scores do not, and how the file gets read.

Two: how to separate personal and business credit so the buyer is not pledging personal lines for business growth and then carrying the personal-credit cost into the next cycle.

Three: how to build the documentation readiness work that the funding file is supposed to carry. EIN aging, entity structure, business banking history, tax filings, revenue evidence. The boring work the lender quietly rewards.

Four: how to walk the lender outreach. The conversation, the file presentation, the questions to ask, the followup discipline.

That is the educational scope. Nothing more.

Who the curriculum is for

Section 3 - Readiness bands (40 to 60 seconds)

There is a free Business Funding Readiness Score on this page. It is 10 to 12 questions. It will route you to one of four bands.

Too Early means the foundation work is not done yet and the curriculum will not pay back at the level you want until the foundation is in place. The score routes you to the free training that handles the foundation.

Foundation-Building means you are at the start of the documentation and credit readiness curve. The entry tier of the curriculum is calibrated for you.

Mentorship-Ready means you have the foundation in place and the mid-tier of the curriculum is the next step.

Advanced-Operator means you have the mentorship work behind you and the operator tier is what comes next. That tier is application-gated.

Take the score before you make a buy decision. If you are Too Early, do not buy the curriculum yet. Build the foundation first.

What you are responsible for

Section 4 - Buyer responsibility (50 to 70 seconds)

The buyer is responsible for the work. The curriculum is the map. The work is yours.

The documentation gets built by you. The credit profile gets built by you. The lender outreach gets executed by you. The discipline to follow through on a 60 to 90 day file build is yours.

If you are looking for a curriculum that does the work for you, the curriculum is not that. If you are looking for a curriculum that hands you a guarantee on a funding decision, the curriculum is not that.

The curriculum is the education. The execution is the buyer.

What the independent lender is responsible for

Section 5 - Underwriting is the gate (40 to 60 seconds)

The funding decision sits with the independent lender. Not with me. Not with the curriculum. The lender reviews the file the buyer presents. The lender decides on the lender's underwriting standards. Those standards depend on creditworthiness, time in business, revenue evidence, documentation completeness, and the specific lender's appetite at the time of the application.

Outcomes vary. The curriculum prepares the buyer for the underwriter conversation. The underwriter decides the outcome.

If anyone in the funding-education category tells you they can guarantee an underwriting outcome, walk away. The underwriter is the gate.

What outcomes vary on

Section 6 - The explicit risk statement (30 to 50 seconds)

Outcomes vary based on the buyer inputs and the independent lender decisions. Specifically:

  • Personal credit profile and history
  • Business credit profile and EIN aging
  • Time in business and revenue evidence
  • Industry and lender risk appetite at the time of the application
  • Buyer discipline on the documentation readiness work

The curriculum will not change the buyer's personal credit history. The curriculum will not change the buyer's time in business. The curriculum will not change the underwriter's decision. The curriculum teaches the buyer how to read the underwriter and how to present the file.

What you do next

Section 7 - The single next step (30 to 40 seconds)

Take the Business Funding Readiness Score on this page. 10 to 12 questions. Four minutes. The score returns a readiness band and a 3-line bottleneck readout. The score routes you to the right next step. If you are Too Early, the routing is the free training. If you are Foundation-Building, Mentorship-Ready, or Advanced-Operator, the routing is the right curriculum tier for the band you are in.

Take the score. Take the readout seriously. Come back to the buy decision after.

Visual treatment notes (for the edit)

  • No B-roll of cars, watches, jewelry, currency, or lifestyle props. The educational-scope register is the cut.
  • Lower-third throughout: "Educational content only. Not financial, legal, or tax advice. Outcomes vary."
  • End card: Take the Readiness Score CTA + chiefsbs.com URL.
  • Captions on by default. AEO benefit (transcript + captions surface for AI engines via VideoObject schema).
  • No music bed during the underwriting / risk statement sections (5 and 6). Cleaner authority signal.
  • One single take preferred per section to keep the operator-educator register intact.

Hard banned in the cut

  • "Trillionaire"
  • "Legally rob the banks"
  • "First Black Trillionaire"
  • "Get $X in Y days"
  • "Guaranteed approval"
  • "Unlock funding"
  • "Bypass the banks"

  • Any timeline tied to a buyer outcome

These belong to the audience-facing register on IG and YouTube. They do not belong in the educational-scope VSL.

Required on screen by end of cut
